Benchmark Reference
The 300 Mt CO₂e threshold is utilized as a sectoral decarbonization indicator, aligned with the IEA's (2023) Net Zero Roadmap and regional 1.5°C "fair share" pathways (e.g., Climate Transparency, 2022), to evaluate a country's trajectory toward mid-century climate neutrality.

UNEP Emissions Gap Report 2024: The annual UNEP report assesses the gap between planned emissions reductions and pathways consistent with Paris Agreement goals. The 2024 edition emphasizes that current NDCs remain insufficient to limit warming to 1.5°C. unep.org

Climate Change Performance Index (CCPI) 2024: Egypt ranks 22nd globally and 2nd in MENA (Middle East & North Africa), based on categories including GHG emissions, renewable energy, energy use, and climate policy. ccpi.org

Growth Metrics (CAGR): Compound Annual Growth Rate calculated as: CAGR = ((End Value / Start Value)1/years - 1) × 100. Used to assess emission trends over multi-year periods.

Egypt's Key Phases:
  • Pre-Arab Spring (2001–2010): Stable emissions trajectory, coal and oil dominance
  • Arab Spring & Reform (2011–2016): Political transition, economic reforms, IMF program initiation
  • Zohr Discovery (2017–present): Major gas field discovery (2015) transformed energy mix, surge in natural gas production, decline in oil share

HDI-Adjusted Emissions: The indicator combines CO₂ per capita with Human Development Index to assess climate justice and equitable development. Based on UNDP's Planetary pressures-adjusted HDI (PHDI) framework which adjusts HDI for environmental pressures. hdr.undp.org
